FAQ's

We are all about saving, so Stasher is free to use. We’re here to help you become master savers and keep your money safe from bad guys.

Secret Agents / Commanders

Q. What is a Secret Agent?
A. A Secret Agent is a kid who saves their money with Stasher. Secret Agents complete Missions, earn rewards and track their savings.
Q. What is a Commander?
A: A Commander is a parent or guardian over the age of 18 who is the overseer of a Secret Agent’s savings account. The Commander links a savings account on behalf of the Secret Agent for him/her to track.
Q. How do Commanders connect with their Secret Agents?
A: A Commander approves and accepts any and all activity of a Secret Agent. A Commander must accept a Secret Agent and a Secret Agent must accept a Commander. Any Secret Agent who registers on Stasher is required to provide a parent’s or guardian’s email address.

Missions

Q. What is a Mission?
A:A Mission is a task, goal or challenge initiated by a Commander for a Secret Agent to complete. Missions have specified timeframes and rewards.
Once a Mission is marked complete by both a Secret Agent and Commander, the reward (if monetary) is automatically transferred from the Commander’s linked bank account to the Secret Agent’s linked savings account.
Q. Does each Mission have to have a monetary reward?
A: Rewards can either be monetary or non-monetary. Non-monetary rewards can be anything, from a new video game, to extra TV time, to a later curfew, to hugs and kisses.

Bank Accounts / Money Transfers

Q. Which bank accounts are used?
A: A Commander links a joint custodial savings account to a Secret Agent’s Stasher account for the Secret Agent to receive money. A Commander also links his/her own bank account to fund any payments to a Secret Agent’s savings account.
Q. What is a joint custodial savings account?
A: A joint custodial savings account is a bank account set up for and on behalf of anyone under the age of 18, typically by a parent or guardian who is an adult. Any money transferred into the account legally belongs to the under-18 recipient. The adult oversees the account and controls any and all account activity.
Q. Who can send and accept money transfers?
A: Only Commanders can transfer and accept money into a Secret Agent’s savings account. Secret Agents cannot transfer money to and from their savings accounts on their own. Secret Agents only observe and monitor the transactions occurring in their savings accounts.
